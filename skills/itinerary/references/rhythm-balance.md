# Rhythm Balance Methodology

## Principle

Travel burnout is real. A good itinerary alternates between high-intensity and low-intensity days to keep energy sustainable across the trip.

## Day Classification

### Heavy Day (Energy: High)
- 3-4 major attractions or activities
- ~15,000-20,000 steps
- 6-8 hours of structured activity
- Best for: city exploration, museum hopping, hiking
- Risk: exhaustion, rushing through things

### Moderate Day (Energy: Medium)
- 2 major + 1 minor activity
- ~10,000-15,000 steps
- 4-6 hours of structured activity
- Best for: mixed sightseeing, one neighborhood deep-dive
- The "normal" day

### Light Day (Energy: Low)
- 1 major activity or 2 minor ones
- ~5,000-10,000 steps
- 2-4 hours of structured activity
- Best for: recovery, spontaneous exploration, café culture
- Essential after: heavy days, travel days, long flights

## Sequencing Rules

1. **Arrival day = light day**. Always. Don't schedule anything that requires a reservation.
2. **After a heavy day, schedule a light day.** No exceptions.
3. **Two heavy days in a row is acceptable** only if the third day is light.
4. **Departure day = light day.** Only morning activity, then travel.
5. **First full day can be heavy** — traveler has the most energy at the start.

## Example 7-Day Rhythm

| Day | Intensity | Type |
|-----|-----------|------|
| 1 | Light | Arrival, neighborhood walk |
| 2 | Heavy | Full city exploration |
| 3 | Moderate | Second area + food focus |
| 4 | Light | Recovery, one highlight + free time |
| 5 | Heavy | Day trip or third area |
| 6 | Moderate | Shopping, culture, farewell dinner |
| 7 | Light | Departure |

## Buffer Time Rules

- **Between activities**: 15-30 minutes (travel + orientation)
- **After lunch**: 30 minutes (food coma is real)
- **Daily flex slot**: 2-3 hours of unscheduled time
- **Emergency buffer**: 1 hour before any timed reservation

## Adjusting for Traveler Type

| Type | Heavy/Week | Light/Week | Notes |
|------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Solo | 3-4 | 2-3 | More flexibility, can push harder |
| Couple | 3 | 3-4 | Balance interests of both |
| Family w/ kids | 2 | 4-5 | Kids need downtime, naps, snacks |
| Group | 2-3 | 3-4 | Coordination overhead adds time |
| Seniors | 2 | 4-5 | More rest, shorter activity blocks |
